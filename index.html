About Return to the Moon

Learn More About NASA's Plan To Put The First Woman On The Moon

With the Artemis program, Nasa will land the first woman on the Moon by 2024, using innovative technologies to explore more of the lunar suraface than ever before. This program is a collaboration between NASA, commercial, and international partners and will establish sustainable exploartion by the end of the decade. Then, NASA will use what they learn on and around the Moon to take the giant leap - sending astronauts to Mars.

Artemis I

Artemis I will be the first integrated test of NASA's deep space exploartion systems: the Orion spacecraft, Space Lanuch System (SLS) rocket and the ground systems at Kennedy Space Center. The first in a series of increasingly complex missions, Artemis I will be an uncrewed flight test that will provide a foundation for human deep space exploration, and demonstrate our commitment and capability to extend human existence to the Moon and beyond.

"This is a mission that truly will do what hasn't been done and learn what isn't known," said Mike Sarafin, Artemis I mission manager at NASA headquarters in Washington. "It will blaze a trail that people will follow on the next Orion flight, pushing the edges of the envelope to prepare for that mission."
